The Shawshank Redemption

"Fear can hold you prisoner. Hope can set you free."

About the Movie

Released in 1994 and based on a Stephen King novella, the film follows Andy Dufresne, a banker wrongly convicted of murdering his wife, as he serves a life sentence at Shawshank State Penitentiary. Over two decades, he builds a quiet friendship with fellow inmate Red and slowly works toward a plan that redefines what it means to hold onto hope in a place built to crush it.

Main Characters

  • Andy Dufresne, a banker sent to Shawshank for a crime he didn't commit.
  • Ellis "Red" Redding, Andy's closest friend and the film's narrator.
  • Warden Samuel Norton, the corrupt head of the prison.
  • Byron Hadley, the brutal chief guard.
  • Brooks Hatlen, the elderly prison librarian.

Movie Timeline

  1. Andy is convicted of murder and sent to Shawshank in 1947.
  2. He befriends Red and starts using his finance background to help the guards with taxes.
  3. Andy builds a relationship with Warden Norton by laundering money for him.
  4. After years of tunneling through his cell wall with a small rock hammer, Andy escapes through the sewage pipe.
  5. Red is paroled, follows Andy's instructions, and the two reunite on a beach in Zihuatanejo.

Interesting Facts

The film was a box office disappointment on release, barely covering its production budget in theaters.

It found its real audience later through cable reruns and home video, eventually becoming one of the most beloved films ever made.

Despite earning seven Academy Award nominations, including Best Picture, the film didn't win a single one, but it now consistently ranks at the top of IMDb's list of the greatest films of all time.
