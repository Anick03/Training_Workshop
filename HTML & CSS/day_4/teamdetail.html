Team History

Portugal made their first World Cup appearance in 1966, finishing in third place behind the legendary performances of Eusébio, who won the tournament's Golden Boot. After a long gap without qualifying, the team returned to consistent World Cup football starting in 2002 and has been a regular presence at the tournament ever since. Their best World Cup finish remains third place from 1966, though they've reached the quarterfinals and knockout rounds multiple times since, including a run to the quarterfinals in 2022.

Memorable World Cup Moments

  1. Eusébio scoring 9 goals in the 1966 tournament, still a Portuguese record
  2. Portugal's dramatic comeback from 3-0 down to draw North Korea 5-3 in 1966
  3. Portugal finishing third at the 1966 World Cup, their best result ever
  4. Cristiano Ronaldo becoming the first player to score in five different World Cups (2022)
  5. Portugal's 6-1 win over Switzerland in the 2022 Round of 16, featuring Gonçalo Ramos's hat trick
